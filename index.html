Five opportunities will define which Saudi banks lead the next decade — and which simply watch it happen. Monetization. Tokenization. Capital Efficiency. AI-Led Sovereign Ecosystem. The Scoreboard. I've modelled the whole thing. Four levers. Three scenarios. One number for the board.

01The opportunity most boards haven't yet sized

Non-interest income is around 25% of Saudi bank operating income. Saudi banks grew net fees and commissions 16.4% in 2024 — the direction is right, the level is still a long way from what the best-run banks in the world run their P&Ls on.

The global picture makes the size of the prize unmistakable. GCC peers like Emirates NBD and FAB are running at roughly 45% non-interest income share. DBS Singapore — the fairest apples-to-apples comparison for a sovereign-backed universal bank in a high-growth economy — is at 40%. JPMorgan Chase runs a 48% fee-led P&L. UBS, the wealth endgame, runs at 75%. Saudi banks are not just behind the region. They are behind the world.

"Close even half the gap to global peers on a SAR 160B sector operating income base and you unlock SAR 29.5 billion of new recurring, capital-light income every year — before the balance sheet moves."

Monetization is the fastest, least capital-intensive route to the P&L Saudi banks are already capable of running. The building blocks exist. The regulation is live. The fee pools are real. What's missing is a named owner, a target number, and a date.

Governance & Regulation

The regulatory frameworks that shape every AI decision

Banks deploying AI at scale must navigate overlapping regulatory regimes across model risk, data privacy, algorithmic accountability, and capital requirements.

Federal Reserve · USA

SR 11-7

Model Risk Management guidance. The global gold standard for model validation, inventory, performance monitoring, and board-level MRM governance.

Applies: All banks using quantitative models for decisions
PRA · UK

SS1/23

Model risk management principles for banks. Extended scope covering AI/ML models, third-party models, and proportionality expectations.

Applies: UK-regulated banks and branches
NIST · USA

AI RMF 1.0

AI Risk Management Framework. Voluntary but increasingly referenced by regulators. Four pillars: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.

Applies: Cross-industry, adopted by forward-thinking FS firms
European Union

EU AI Act

World's first comprehensive AI regulation. Risk-tiered approach with strict requirements for high-risk AI systems including credit scoring and insurance pricing.

Applies: Any AI system serving EU citizens
SDAIA · KSA

NDMO & PDPL

Saudi National Data Management Office governance framework and Personal Data Protection Law. Mandatory for all entities processing data in the Kingdom.

Applies: All Saudi-operating financial institutions
Basel Committee

Basel IV

Revised standardised approaches and output floors. Directly impacts RWA calculations and capital efficiency — a core lever in the Scoreboard model.

Applies: Globally significant banks, phased 2025–2028
CBUAE · UAE

AI & ML Guidance

Published February 2026. Consumer protection and responsible AI adoption framework for all UAE-licensed financial institutions. Five pillars: governance and accountability, fairness and non-discrimination, transparency and explainability, effective human oversight, and data management and privacy.

Applies: All licensed financial institutions in the UAE
