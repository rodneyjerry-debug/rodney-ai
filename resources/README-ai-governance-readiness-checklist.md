A comprehensive 40-point assessment framework for financial institutions to evaluate their AI governance maturity against leading regulatory standards and best practices.

## Overview

The AI Governance Readiness Checklist is a professional assessment tool designed to help financial institutions systematically evaluate and improve their AI governance capabilities. This checklist integrates requirements from four major regulatory and standards frameworks:

- **SR 11-7**: Federal Reserve Guidance on Model Risk Management
- **SS1/23**: PRA Supervisory Statement on Operational Resilience (UK)
- **NIST AI RMF**: National Institute of Standards & Technology AI Risk Management Framework
- **EU AI Act**: European Union Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation 2024/1689)

---

## What This Checklist Covers

### Seven Governance Domains

The assessment is organized into seven complementary domains covering the full AI governance lifecycle:

#### 1. **Policy & Strategy** (8 items)
Foundational governance framework and strategic alignment
- Board-approved AI governance charter
- AI risk appetite statement
- Comprehensive AI policy framework
- Three lines of defense model
- Board and management reporting
- AI ethics framework
- Model inventory and registry
- Change management procedures

#### 2. **Model Risk Management / SR 11-7** (8 items)
Development, validation, and monitoring of AI models
- Model development standards and lifecycle governance
- Independent model validation and sign-off
- Backtesting and performance monitoring
- Model documentation and explainability
- Stress testing and scenario analysis
- Model risk capital allocation
- Third-party/vendor model governance
- Incident management and remediation

#### 3. **SS1/23 Compliance (UK PRA)** (6 items)
Operational resilience specific to AI systems
- Senior manager accountability (SMAR designation)
- Operational resilience impact tolerance
- AI model resilience testing
- Data quality and availability controls
- Fallback arrangements and override procedures
- Regulatory reporting to PRA

#### 4. **NIST AI RMF Alignment** (6 items)
Integration with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- AI RMF governance and accountability
- Risk mapping (MAP function)
- Risk measurement and metrics (MEASURE function)
- Risk mitigation and controls (MANAGE function)
- Continuous monitoring (MONITOR function)
- Enterprise risk management integration

#### 5. **EU AI Act Readiness** (6 items)
Preparation for and compliance with EU AI Act requirements
- AI system classification by risk level
- Risk assessment for high-risk systems
- Compliance with prohibited AI practices
- Transparency and disclosure for AI decisions
- Governance and compliance management system
- Conformity assessment and CE marking readiness

#### 6. **Data Governance & Quality** (6 items)
Data management and quality throughout the AI lifecycle
- Data governance framework and ownership
- Data quality monitoring and validation
- Bias detection and mitigation
- Data lineage and provenance documentation
- Privacy and consent management
- Data retention and deletion policies

#### 7. **People, Culture & Training** (4 items)
Organizational capabilities and awareness
- AI skills assessment and hiring
- Regular training programs on AI risks
- Clear accountabilities and escalation procedures
- AI ethics and responsible AI framework

**Total: 44 governance checkpoints**

## How to Use This Checklist

### Step 1: Assemble Your Team
Gather representatives from key functions:
- **Chief Risk Officer / Risk Management**
- **Head of AI / Data Science**
- **Compliance and Regulatory Affairs**
- **Model Risk Management**
- **Technology and Data Engineering**
- **Business Unit Leaders** (where AI systems are deployed)

### Step 2: Complete the Assessment
For each of the 44 items:
1. **Read** the item description and regulatory reference
2. **Evaluate** your institution's current state against the definition
3. **Score** from 1-5 using the maturity scale (see below)
4. **Document** evidence supporting your score
5. **Note** any regulatory references for compliance mapping

**Estimated completion time: 45-60 minutes**

### Step 3: Calculate Domain and Overall Scores
1. Average the scores within each governance domain to obtain domain-level maturity (e.g., Policy & Strategy average)
2. Average all item scores to calculate your overall governance maturity score
3. Record results in the Summary Scorecard template

### Step 4: Identify Priority Gaps
1. List items scoring 1-2 (significant gaps requiring urgent attention)
2. Prioritize by regulatory impact and operational risk
3. Focus on high-risk domains first (typically Model Risk Management and Policy & Strategy)

### Step 5: Develop Your Remediation Roadmap
Use the scoring results to plan improvements:
- **Immediate (0-3 months)**: Address critical gaps, establish foundational governance framework
- **Near-term (3-6 months)**: Complete policy suite, implement core controls in high-risk domains
- **Medium-term (6-12 months)**: Build advanced capabilities (monitoring, bias detection, model optimization)
- **Ongoing**: Maintain alignment with evolving regulations and emerging best practices

### Step 6: Engage for Advisory Support (Optional)
Consider Rodney.AI advisory support for:
- Strategic governance planning and framework development
- Control design and implementation
- Staff training and capability building
- Regulatory engagement and documentation
- Remediation roadmap execution

## Scoring Methodology

### Maturity Scale (1-5)

| Score | Level | Definition |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **1** | Initial/Ad-hoc | Practice is not in place or only ad-hoc; significant work required |
| **2** | Developing | Practice is partially implemented; many gaps remain |
| **3** | Defined | Practice is documented and implemented; some gaps in consistency or coverage |
| **4** | Managed | Practice is well-implemented and monitored; minor gaps only |
| **5** | Optimized | Practice is fully implemented, monitored, and continuously improved |

### Scoring Guidelines

**Score 1 (Initial)**: 
- No formal process exists
- Only isolated, manual efforts
- No documentation or standardization
- Example: Ad-hoc model validation with no formal sign-off

**Score 2 (Developing)**:
- Basic process exists but incomplete
- Some documentation, but gaps remain
- Limited application across the organization
- Example: Model validation process exists but validation criteria not fully documented

**Score 3 (Defined)**:
- Well-documented, standardized process
- Generally applied across organization
- Some gaps in coverage or monitoring
- Example: Formal model validation framework with documented criteria; applied to most models

**Score 4 (Managed)**:
- Process is well-established and monitored
- Strong controls and oversight
- Consistent application with minor exceptions
- Example: Formal validation framework with metrics, dashboard monitoring, quarterly reviews

**Score 5 (Optimized)**:
- Process is fully mature, automated where possible
- Continuous improvement mechanisms
- Industry best practice level
- Example: Automated validation framework with real-time monitoring, machine learning optimization, continuous feedback loops

### Calculating Results

**Domain Score** = Average of all item scores within that domain

Example for Policy & Strategy (8 items):
- Items scored: 4, 3, 3, 4, 2, 2, 3, 4
- Domain Score = (4+3+3+4+2+2+3+4) / 8 = **3.125**

**Overall Governance Maturity Score** = Average of all 44 item scores

## Regulatory Framework Reference

### SR 11-7 Coverage
SR 11-7 requires banks to have appropriate governance for model risk management, including:
- Board and senior management oversight
- Independent validation of models
- Ongoing monitoring and performance tracking
- Comprehensive documentation
- Escalation procedures for model failures

This checklist covers all SR 11-7 requirements, primarily in the Model Risk Management domain, with supporting items in Policy & Strategy.

### SS1/23 Coverage
SS1/23 requires UK PRA firms to ensure operational resilience of critical functions, including AI-dependent systems:
- Senior manager accountability
- Impact tolerance definition and testing
- Scenario testing and recovery planning
- Data quality and availability management

Relevant items are concentrated in the SS1/23 Compliance domain.

### NIST AI RMF Coverage
The NIST AI Risk Management Framework provides a flexible approach to AI risk management through four functions:
- **MAP**: Understand context and risks
- **MEASURE**: Measure performance and risks
- **MANAGE**: Implement controls and mitigations
- **MONITOR**: Track and improve continuously

This checklist aligns the seven domains with the NIST AI RMF structure, with dedicated items in the NIST AI RMF Alignment domain.

### EU AI Act Coverage
The EU AI Act establishes a risk-based approach to regulating AI systems:
- Prohibited practices (Article 5)
- High-risk classification and controls (Articles 6-15)
- Transparency requirements (Articles 13-14)
- Governance and compliance (Articles 16-29)
- Conformity assessment (Articles 43-49)

Dedicated coverage in the EU AI Act Readiness domain, with supporting items across other domains.

## Implementation Tips

### Getting Started
1. **Don't overthink it**: Score based on your honest assessment of current state, not aspirational future state
2. **Evidence matters**: Document evidence supporting each score (policies, procedures, audit reports, metrics)
3. **Cross-functional input**: Scores should reflect consensus across Risk, Compliance, Technology, and Business teams
4. **Benchmark internally**: Compare scores across different AI systems or business units to identify pockets of best practice

### Moving Forward
1. **Start with foundations**: Items scoring 1-2 in Policy & Strategy should be first priority
2. **Leverage existing frameworks**: Build on existing risk governance, data governance, or compliance frameworks
3. **Invest in people**: Skills gaps often underlie low scores; training and hiring are critical enablers
4. **Use data**: Implement monitoring and dashboards to support ongoing assessment and improvement
5. **Stay current**: Regulatory landscape is evolving; plan for EU AI Act, upcoming NIST updates, and other emerging frameworks
