# Banking AI Readiness Scorecard

## Overview

The **Banking AI Readiness Scorecard** is a comprehensive Excel workbook designed to benchmark your bank's AI maturity across six critical capability dimensions. It includes peer comparison data, a professional dashboard with visualizations, and an action planning framework to prioritize your AI transformation initiatives.

**Target Audience:** C-suite executives, Chief Data Officers, Chief Information Officers, AI Steering Committee members, and Digital Transformation leads at financial services institutions in the Middle East and globally.

---

## Key Features

### Six Capability Dimensions

1. **Data Maturity** — Data quality, lineage, cataloging, access, real-time pipelines, and privacy compliance
2. **AI Talent & Skills** — Data scientists, ML engineers, business translators, upskilling programs, retention, and org structure
3. **Governance & Risk** — AI policy, model risk management, ethics, regulatory alignment, audit, and incident management
4. **Technology & Infrastructure** — Cloud, MLOps, compute resources, APIs, security, and observability
5. **Use Case Portfolio** — Pipeline management, production models, ROI tracking, scaling capability, innovation, and GenAI adoption
6. **Operating Model** — CoE structure, centralized vs federated decisions, vendor management, budgeting, executive sponsorship, and change management

Each dimension contains 6-8 detailed sub-criteria for granular assessment.

### Scoring Framework (1-5 Scale)

- **1 = Ad Hoc:** No formal processes or governance; manual, inconsistent execution
- **2 = Basic:** Initial processes in place; some standardization but significant gaps
- **3 = Structured:** Documented processes and standards; effective execution in most areas
- **4 = Advanced:** Mature, optimized processes; strong governance and execution
- **5 = Leading:** Industry-leading capabilities; continuous innovation and best practice

## Workbook Sheets

### 1. **Instructions**
- Overview of the scorecard's purpose and how to use it
- Scoring scale definitions
- Dimension descriptions
- Peer benchmark context

### 2. **Assessment** (Main Scoring Sheet)
- **Your Input Areas:**
  - **Current Score (1-5):** Your bank's present capability level for each sub-criterion
  - **Target Score (1-5):** Where you aspire to be
  - **Evidence/Notes:** Justification for your score and key observations

- **Automatic Calculations:**
  - **Gap:** Target minus Current (identifies priority areas)
  - **Dimension Averages:** Aggregated scores across all sub-criteria
  - **Overall Readiness Score:** Mean of all six dimension averages

- **Features:**
  - Dropdown menus (1-5) for easy data entry
  - Color-coded input cells for clarity
  - Formula-driven gap calculations (no manual math required)
  - Professional formatting aligned with Rodney.AI brand

### 3. **Peer Benchmark**
Pre-filled comparison data to contextualize your assessment:
- **Industry Average:** Overall banking sector baseline
- **Leading GCC Bank:** Top performer in Middle East region
- **Top-5 UK Bank:** Leading UK financial institution
- **Digital-Native (e.g., DBS):** Benchmark for tech-forward Asian bank

Compare your current scores against these peers to identify competitive positioning.

### 4. **Dashboard**
Professional analytics view featuring:
- **Overall Readiness Score:** Single KPI at a glance
- **Dimension Summary Table:** Current, target, and gap scores for all six areas
- **Radar Chart:** Visual comparison of current vs. target across dimensions
- **Gap Bar Chart:** Identifies which dimensions need the most improvement

Charts auto-update when you modify assessment scores.

### 5. **Action Plan**
Template for turning gaps into priority actions:
- **Dimension:** Which capability area
- **Priority:** High/Medium/Low
- **Action:** Specific initiative or project
- **Owner/Team:** Accountability
- **Timeline:** Target completion date
- **Status:** Not Started / In Progress / Complete

Six example actions provided; expand as needed.

### 6. **Glossary**
Detailed 1-5 scoring definitions for each dimension, helping assessors calibrate their judgments and maintain consistency.

## How to Use This Scorecard

### Step 1: Prepare Your Team
Gather representatives from key functions:
- Data Engineering / Analytics
- AI/ML Organization
- Risk & Compliance
- Technology Operations
- Business Unit Leaders

A 2-3 hour facilitated session works well to align on scores.

### Step 2: Score the Assessment Sheet
1. Open the **Assessment** sheet
2. For each sub-criterion, discuss and enter:
   - **Current Score** (1-5) using the dropdown
   - **Target Score** (1-5) for your 18-24 month vision
   - **Evidence/Notes** (e.g., "Have 3 data scientists, need 8"; "No formal MRM process in place yet")
3. Gaps and dimension averages calculate automatically
4. Overall readiness score updates in real-time

### Step 3: Review Peer Benchmarks
- Navigate to **Peer Benchmark** sheet
- Compare your current scores to industry averages and peers
- Identify where you stand relative to competitors
- Use this context to calibrate targets (should they be 4.0 or 5.0?)

### Step 4: Analyze the Dashboard
- View your **Overall Readiness Score** at a glance
- Study the **radar chart** to spot dimension imbalances
- Use the **gap bar chart** to prioritize which areas need focus
- Dimensions with largest gaps = highest priority

### Step 5: Create Your Action Plan
1. Open the **Action Plan** sheet
2. List your top 10-15 initiatives, linked to gap areas
3. For each action, define:
   - Owner and team
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Success metrics
4. Sequence actions by priority and dependencies
5. Use this as your transformation roadmap

### Step 6: Share & Present
- Use the Dashboard sheet for executive presentations
- Share the full workbook with steering committee for governance
- Update scores quarterly/annually to track progress

## Practical Tips

### Scoring Honestly
- Avoid over-optimistic or overly pessimistic assessments
- Base scores on evidence: documented processes, team feedback, audit reports
- Be specific in Evidence/Notes to support your rationale
- Remember: this is not an audit or compliance check—it's a self-assessment to drive improvement

### Benchmarking Strategy
- Your scores should reflect your current state, not aspirations
- Peer data is for context only; don't adjust your scores to match peers
- Digital-native banks (e.g., DBS at 4.5) are often 2-3 years ahead; tailor targets accordingly

### Linking to Corporate Strategy
- Ensure AI targets (in the Target Score column) align with your 3-year business strategy
- Prioritize actions that unlock revenue (new products, customer insights) vs. cost reduction
- Consider regulatory mandates (e.g., GenAI governance) when setting timelines

### Updating & Tracking
- Schedule quarterly reviews to update scores and track progress
- A move from 2.0 to 3.0 typically takes 6-12 months; be realistic
- Use the Action Plan sheet to monitor delivery
