AI Risk Taxonomy for Banking

A categorisation of 30+ AI risks across six dimensions, mapped to existing risk frameworks and to the controls that address them. Use this as a starting point for your own AI risk register.

1 · Data risk

RiskDescriptionPrimary control
Data quality & lineageModels trained on stale, incomplete or unrepresentative data.Data quality SLAs, lineage tooling
Sensitive data exposurePII, PCI or material non-public information leaking into training sets or prompts.Data classification, DLP, prompt scanning
Bias in training dataHistorical bias encoded into model behavior.Fairness testing, representative sampling
Concept & data driftReal-world data diverges from training distribution.Drift monitoring, retraining triggers
Synthetic / poisoned dataAdversarial or fabricated data corrupting models.Provenance, anomaly detection

2 · Model risk

RiskDescriptionPrimary control
Model error / inaccuracyPredictions outside acceptable error bounds.Validation, challenger models
Hallucination (GenAI)LLMs producing confident but false outputs.Grounding, retrieval, human review
Explainability gapInability to explain a model decision to a customer or regulator.Interpretability tooling, simpler challengers
Model bias / unfair outcomesDisparate impact across protected groups.Fairness metrics, post-hoc adjustments
Robustness & adversarialSensitivity to adversarial inputs or prompt injection.Red-teaming, input filtering

3 · Deployment & operational risk

RiskDescriptionPrimary control
Shadow AIBusiness teams using AI tools outside IT/risk visibility.AI inventory, gateway, allowlists
Deployment failureProduction model performs differently to validation.Canary, A/B, shadow deployment
Inadequate monitoringRisks materialising undetected post-deployment.SLOs, metric monitoring, alerting
Cost & compute concentrationRunaway inference cost; reliance on a single GPU vendor.FinOps, multi-vendor strategy

4 · Third-party & supply chain risk

RiskDescriptionPrimary control
Foundation model dependencyMaterial dependence on a single LLM provider.Multi-model architecture, abstraction layer
Vendor opacityInability to assess training data, fine-tuning, evals.Vendor questionnaires, contractual rights
Data residency & cross-borderSensitive data routed to non-compliant jurisdictions.Regional endpoints, contractual controls

5 · Conduct, ethical & reputational risk

RiskDescriptionPrimary control

RiskDescriptionPrimary control
EU AI Act non-complianceFailure to meet obligations for high-risk / GPAI systems.Inventory, classification, conformity

IP infringementTraining data or outputs infringing copyright.Indemnities, content filters
Privacy / GDPRLawful basis, automated decision rights.DPIAs, opt-out, human review
Cross-border supervisory divergenceConflicting expectations across jurisdictions.Most-restrictive baseline approach

How to use this taxonomy

  1. Map each row to your existing operational, model and conduct risk frameworks. Avoid creating a parallel risk universe.
  2. Score each risk for inherent and residual impact at the institution level.
  3. For each AI use case, identify which subset applies and confirm controls in place.
  4. Aggregate at portfolio level for the AI Risk Committee dashboard.
  5. Refresh annually — and after every material AI incident or regulatory change.
