AI Risk Taxonomy for Banking

A categorisation of 30+ AI risks across six dimensions, mapped to existing risk frameworks and to the controls that address them. Use this as a starting point for your own AI risk register.

1 · Data risk

Data is the foundation of all AI models. Weakness here propagates through the entire system. Eight distinct risks:

Risk appetite statement template

A board-approved risk appetite statement for AI should define tolerance by tier and risk category. Here is a template:

  • Tier 1 (Critical systems): We will accept only residual risk in the 'low' range. All critical controls must be independent and operational before deployment. Annual revalidation required. Zero tolerance for undetected biases or regulatory violations.
  • Tier 2 (High systems): We will accept residual risk in the 'low to moderate' range. Primary controls must be operational; secondary controls can be phased in post-deployment. Semi-annual revalidation. Biases monitored and remediated within 30 days.
  • Tier 3 (Moderate systems): We will accept residual risk in the 'moderate' range. Self-assessment controls acceptable; escalation if material exceptions found. Annual review. Fairness audits on sampling basis.
  • Tier 4 (Low systems): We will accept residual risk in the 'moderate to higher' range. Self-attestation sufficient; no specific oversight required unless concerns raised.
  • Data risk: We will not accept residual risk of PII/sensitive data exposure beyond 'low'. We will not accept residual bias in lending, pricing or hiring models beyond 'low'. Drift in critical models must be detected within one business day.
  • Operational risk: We will not accept more than 10% of our AI portfolio in 'shadow' (unregistered) mode at any time. GenAI usage must flow through our enterprise gateway. Critical system downtime beyond 4 hours triggers incident escalation.
  • Third-party risk: We will not depend on a single foundation model provider for more than 30% of our GenAI workload. All Tier 1 vendors must have current SOC 2 Type II attestation.

How to use this taxonomy

  1. Map to existing frameworks. Don't create a parallel risk universe. Map each risk category to your operational, model and conduct risk classifications in your existing risk register.
  2. Score inherent & residual risk. For each risk category, estimate inherent (no controls) and residual (with controls in place) impact & likelihood. Use a 5x5 matrix (impact: low–critical; likelihood: rare–almost certain).
  3. Identify controls. For each identified risk, confirm which primary and secondary controls are in place. Track exceptions and remediation.
  4. Use case risk assessment. For each AI use case intake, identify which subset of the taxonomy applies. Confirm controls are in scope for that use case's risk tier.
  5. Portfolio aggregation. Aggregate risk scores to portfolio level. Feed into AI Risk Committee dashboard and board reporting.
  6. Refresh & improve. Update the taxonomy annually, and after every material AI incident or major regulatory change. Include lessons from near-misses.
