## Overview

A practical implementation guide for the EU AI Act, the world's first comprehensive AI regulation. Maps compliance requirements to implementation timeline and provides checklists for prohibited, high-risk, and limited-risk AI systems.

## What's Included

- **EU AI Act Overview**: Key dates, scope, and regulatory approach
- **Risk-Based Classification**: How to categorize your AI systems
- **Implementation Timeline**: Phased approach aligned with regulatory milestones
- **Compliance Checklist**: Specific requirements for each risk category
- **Gap Analysis Framework**: Assess your current vs. required state
- **Organizational Requirements**: Governance, documentation, roles

## AI System Risk Classification

**Prohibited**: Banned entirely (social scoring, mass surveillance)  
**High-Risk**: Extensive requirements (recruitment, credit decisions, biometric ID)  
**Limited Risk**: Transparency requirements (chatbots, interactive systems)  
**Minimal/No Risk**: Few requirements (most other systems)  

## High-Risk AI Systems in Financial Services

- Credit/loan decisions
- Recruitment and employment
- Biometric identification
- Credit scoring
- Employment contract decisions
- Benefit eligibility
- Trading and algorithmic management

## Compliance Requirements by Phase

**Phase 1 (Now - Dec 2024): Assessment**
- Map all AI systems
- Classify by risk level
- Identify compliance gaps
- Plan remediation approach

**Phase 2 (Jan - Jun 2025): Design**
- Document AI system design
- Implement required controls
- Build governance processes
- Train teams on requirements

**Phase 3 (Jul 2025 - Jan 2026): Implementation**
- Deploy control systems
- Complete testing and validation
- Prepare for audits
- Establish monitoring

**Phase 4 (Feb 2026+): Compliance**
- Maintain ongoing compliance
- Monitor regulatory changes
- Conduct audits
- Report as required

## Key Compliance Elements

**Documentation**: Detailed records of AI system development and performance  
**Data Governance**: Documentation of training data and quality  
**Bias Testing**: Regular testing for discrimination and fairness  
**Human Oversight**: Procedures for human review of AI decisions  
**Transparency**: Clear disclosure when AI is being used  
**Performance Monitoring**: Tracking system performance in production  
**Audit Trails**: Complete logs of AI system decisions (1+ year)  

## Governance Requirements

Your organization must establish:
- **Clear AI governance structure** with assigned responsibilities
- **Documented policies** for AI development and deployment
- **Audit procedures** for compliance validation
- **Incident management** for AI-related issues
- **Board-level oversight** of high-risk systems
- **Regular training** for staff developing/deploying AI

## Resource Estimate

- **Compliance team**: 1-2 FTE ongoing
- **Technical implementation**: $500K-$2M depending on scope
- **Training and awareness**: $100K-$200K
- **Legal and audit support**: $200K-$500K
- **Total Year 1**: $1M-$3M typical organization

## Risk of Non-Compliance

- **Fines**: Up to 6% of global revenue or €30M (whichever is higher)
- **Operational impact**: Systems must be taken offline if non-compliant
- **Reputational damage**: Public disclosure of violations
- **Customer impact**: Loss of customer trust and business
- **Liability exposure**: Civil lawsuits from affected parties
