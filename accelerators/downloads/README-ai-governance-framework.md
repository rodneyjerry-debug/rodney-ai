# AI Governance Framework for Financial Services

**Document ID:** ai-governance-framework.pdf  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** April 2025

## Overview

This comprehensive framework establishes governance structures, decision-making authority, and accountability mechanisms for AI systems in financial services organizations. It provides templates for policies, RACI matrices, and committee charters that can be adapted to any FS firm.

## What's Included

- **Governance Structure**: Council, committee, and board relationships
- **Three-Tier Committee System**: Strategic council, implementation committee, ethics board
- **Policy Templates**: AI development, data governance, and risk management policies
- **RACI Matrices**: Clear accountability for all major AI activities
- **Committee Terms of Reference**: Charters with specific responsibilities
- **Decision-Making Framework**: Approval thresholds by initiative type
- **Monitoring & Reporting**: KPIs and board-level metrics

## Target Audience

- Chief Risk Officer
- Chief Information Officer
- Chief Compliance Officer
- Board Risk Committee Members
- AI Governance Council Members

## How to Use This Document

1. **Establish your governance council** using the structure provided
2. **Adapt the policy templates** to your organization's risk profile
3. **Assign accountabilities** using the RACI matrix as a template
4. **Define committees** with the Terms of Reference framework
5. **Set decision authorities** based on investment thresholds
6. **Implement monitoring** using the KPI framework

## Key Governance Elements

- **AI Governance Council**: Monthly oversight of strategic initiatives and risk
- **AI Implementation Committee**: Bi-weekly operational management
- **AI Ethics & Fairness Board**: Quarterly review of bias and ethical issues
- **Clear escalation paths** for issues and conflicts
- **Board-level reporting** on AI risks and opportunities

## Regulatory Alignment

This framework aligns with:
- Federal Reserve SR 11-7 (Model Risk Management)
- OCC Bulletin 2020-4 (AI/ML Governance)
- FDIC Model Risk Management Guidelines
- EU AI Act (if applicable)
- Consumer Financial Protection Bureau guidance
