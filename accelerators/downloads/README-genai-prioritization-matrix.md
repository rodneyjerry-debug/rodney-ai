**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** April 2025

## Overview

A systematic framework for evaluating and prioritizing GenAI opportunities. Most organizations identify 50-100 potential use cases but can only implement a few. This framework ensures you choose high-value, achievable opportunities that deliver strategic impact.

## What's Included

- **Prioritization Framework**: 4-dimensional scoring approach
- **Evaluation Criteria**: Specific scoring metrics for each dimension
- **Scoring Methodology**: How to calculate overall priority scores
- **Use Case Portfolio**: Sample 20+ GenAI opportunities for evaluation
- **Prioritization Example**: Walk-through of scoring one use case
- **Implementation Roadmap**: How to phase implementation by priority

## Target Audience

- Chief Technology Officer
- Head of AI/ML
- VP of Business Operations
- Use Case Owners (line of business leads)
- AI Steering Committee
- Innovation Teams

## How to Use This Document

1. **Identify 30-50 potential use cases** through workshops
2. **Score each use case** across 4 dimensions
3. **Rank by overall priority score** (4.5-5.0 = implement now)
4. **Group by phase** based on feasibility and dependencies
5. **Build implementation roadmap** with resource plan
6. **Execute Phase 1 quick wins** to build momentum

## The 4-Dimension Prioritization Framework

**Business Value (40%)**
- Financial impact (cost reduction, revenue uplift)
- ROI timeline
- Customer impact and strategic importance

**Feasibility (25%)**
- Technical complexity
- Resource requirements
- Implementation timeline

**Data Readiness (20%)**
- Data availability
- Data quality and completeness
- Integration requirements

**Strategic Fit (15%)**
- Alignment with organizational strategy
- Competitive differentiation
- Risk vs. reward profile

## Scoring Methodology

**Dimension Score**: 1 (low) to 5 (high)  
**Overall Score** = (BV × 0.40) + (Feasibility × 0.25) + (Data × 0.20) + (Strategic × 0.15)  
**Total Range**: 1.0-5.0

**Priority Grouping**
- **4.5-5.0**: Quick Wins - Implement in Phase 1 (0-3 months)
- **3.5-4.4**: High Value - Phase 2 (3-6 months)
- **2.5-3.4**: Medium Value - Phase 3 (6-12 months)
- **<2.5**: Low Priority - Defer or reject

## Evaluation Criteria: Business Value (40%)

| Score | Annual Impact | ROI Timeline | Customer/Strategic Impact |
|-------|--------------|-------------|--------------------------|
| **5** | >$10M | <12 months | Transformational, brand-defining |
| **4** | $3-10M | 12-18 months | Significant improvement, competitive edge |
| **3** | $1-3M | 18-24 months | Moderate improvement |
| **2** | $100K-1M | >24 months | Incremental benefit |
| **1** | <$100K | Unknown | Minimal impact |

## Evaluation Criteria: Feasibility (25%)

| Score | Complexity | Resource Needs | Timeline | Barriers |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|----------|----------|
| **5** | Low | Small team, <$250K | 3-4 months | None significant |
| **4** | Moderate | Team + specialists, $250-500K | 6 months | Minor integration work |
| **3** | Challenging | Large team, $500K-1M | 9 months | Significant customization |
| **2** | Complex | Specialized team, $1-2M | 12-15 months | Major technical challenges |
| **1** | Very High | Major effort, $2M+ | >18 months | Possibly infeasible |

## Evaluation Criteria: Data Readiness (20%)

| Score | Data Availability | Data Quality | Integration |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|
| **5** | Complete, current | Excellent (90%+) | Minimal work |
| **4** | Mostly available | Good (80-90%) | Some integration |
| **3** | Partial | Fair (70-80%) | Significant work |
| **2** | Limited | Poor (<70%) | Major ETL effort |
| **1** | Not available | Very poor | Impossible now |

## Evaluation Criteria: Strategic Fit (15%)

| Score | Strategy Alignment | Competitive Advantage | Risk Management |
|-------|------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **5** | Core to strategy | Clear differentiation | Low risk |
| **4** | Supports strategy | Competitive edge | Moderate risk |
| **3** | Aligned | Incremental edge | Manageable risk |
| **2** | Tangential | Minimal advantage | Higher risk |
| **1** | Conflicting | No advantage | Significant risk |

Plot use cases on a matrix:

**Quadrants**

## Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Quick Wins (Months 0-3)
**Characteristics**
- High business value (Score 4.5+)
- Low complexity (3-4 month timeline)
- Data readily available
- Cross-functional support

**Typical Scope**: 2-3 use cases  
**Example**: Chatbot, content generation, invoice processing  
**Expected Value**: $2-5M annual impact  
**Goals**: Prove concept, generate ROI, build momentum

**Team**: Core AI team (3-5 people)  
**Budget**: $500K-1M  

### Phase 2: Strategic Initiatives (Months 4-9)
**Characteristics**
- Good business value (Score 3.5-4.4)
- Moderate complexity (6-9 months)
- Some data work required
- Higher strategic importance

**Typical Scope**: 3-5 use cases  
**Example**: Advanced analytics, complex workflows  
**Expected Value**: $5-15M annual impact  
**Goals**: Build organizational capability, create competitive edge

**Team**: Expanded AI team (5-10 people)  
**Budget**: $1-2M  

### Phase 3: Transformation (Months 10-18)
**Characteristics**
- Strategic priority (Score 2.5-3.4)
- Higher complexity (9-12 months+)
- Significant data work
- Organizational transformation impact

**Typical Scope**: 3-5 use cases  
**Example**: Core process transformation, new capabilities  
**Expected Value**: $10-30M+ annual impact  
**Goals**: Organizational transformation, market leadership

**Team**: Large AI team (10-20 people)  
**Budget**: $2-5M+  

## Portfolio Management

**Balanced Portfolio Approach**
- **25% Quick Wins**: Fast ROI, momentum, learning
- **50% Strategic**: Medium difficulty, good value
- **25% Transformation**: Long-term competitive advantage

**Resource Allocation**
- **Phase 1**: 60% of team
- **Phase 2**: 30% of team
- **Phase 3**: 10% of team (planning/piloting)

**Dependency Management**
- Identify dependencies between use cases
- Front-load foundation/platform work
- Sequence for optimal learning transfer
- Build reusable components and patterns

## Success Metrics

**Execution Metrics**
- Time to deployment (target: within timeline)
- Budget variance (target: ±10%)
- Team utilization (target: 80%+)

**Business Metrics**
- Actual value realized (target: 90%+ of projection)
- ROI (target: >100% in Year 1)
- Adoption rate (target: 80%+ user adoption)

**Strategic Metrics**
- Competitive advantage achieved
- Organizational capability built
- Culture shift toward AI-first thinking

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Selecting too many use cases**: Start with 2-3, not 10
2. **Underestimating complexity**: Double your timeline estimate
3. **Overestimating business impact**: Validate assumptions with business
4. **Poor data readiness**: Do data assessment before selection
5. **Weak executive sponsorship**: Need C-level support
6. **Lack of change management**: Users must buy in to adoption
7. **No monitoring/optimization**: Business value diminishes over time
