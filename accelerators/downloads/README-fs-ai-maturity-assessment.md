# FS AI Maturity Assessment Guide

**Document ID:** fs-ai-maturity-assessment.pdf  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** April 2025

## Overview

A comprehensive maturity assessment framework for measuring organizational readiness and capability across 7 key dimensions of AI adoption in financial services. Includes scoring methodology, peer benchmarks, and improvement roadmaps.

## What's Included

- **Assessment Framework**: 7 dimensions with 4-5 capability statements each
- **Maturity Levels**: 5-point scale from Initial to Optimized
- **Scoring Guide**: Methodology for assessing current state
- **Benchmark Data**: Peer comparison against 200+ FS organizations
- **Improvement Roadmap**: Phased path from current to target maturity
- **Implementation Planning**: How to act on assessment results

## Target Audience

- Chief Technology Officer
- Chief Risk Officer
- VP of Data & Analytics
- Head of AI/ML
- Strategy & Transformation Leaders
- Board Technology Committee

## How to Use This Document

1. **Gather stakeholders** (8-10 people from business, tech, risk)
2. **Review capability statements** for each dimension
3. **Assess current state** on 1-5 scale
4. **Discuss disagreements** and align on scores
5. **Calculate dimension averages** and overall score
6. **Benchmark against peers** using provided data
7. **Develop improvement plan** using the roadmap

## Assessment Process

**Time Required**: 2-3 hours (initial assessment)  
**Frequency**: Annually or after major implementations  
**Facilitator**: Internal (with guide) or external consultant  
**Outputs**: Maturity profile, benchmark comparison, improvement roadmap  

## Seven Assessment Dimensions

### 1. AI Strategy & Governance
How clearly has AI strategy been defined and implemented?

**Level 1**: Ad-hoc explorations, no formal strategy  
**Level 2**: Some business units exploring AI, informal governance  
**Level 3**: Documented strategy, governance council, annual roadmap  
**Level 4**: Clear strategy aligned with business, executive oversight  
**Level 5**: AI embedded in all strategies, board oversight, continuous optimization  

### 2. Data & Infrastructure
Is your data and technology infrastructure ready for AI?

**Level 1**: Data in silos, inconsistent quality, limited infrastructure  
**Level 2**: Data in multiple systems, some governance, basic infrastructure  
**Level 3**: Centralized data platform, governance policies, cloud infrastructure  
**Level 4**: Enterprise data platform, strong governance, scalable infrastructure  
**Level 5**: Real-time data platform, automated governance, world-class infrastructure  

### 3. Talent & Organization
Do you have the people and structure for AI success?

**Level 1**: Few AI specialists, limited expertise  
**Level 2**: Some specialized hires, emerging AI function  
**Level 3**: Dedicated AI team, recruiting actively, training programs  
**Level 4**: Strong AI center of excellence, competitive compensation, partnerships  
**Level 5**: World-class AI talent, university partnerships, thought leadership  

### 4. Risk Management
Are risks properly identified and controlled?

**Level 1**: Minimal AI-specific risk management  
**Level 2**: Basic risk assessments, informal controls  
**Level 3**: Documented risk framework, policies, monitoring  
**Level 4**: Comprehensive risk management, advanced controls, regular audits  
**Level 5**: Proactive risk management, predictive controls, continuous improvement  

### 5. Use Case Portfolio
How mature is your portfolio of AI applications?

**Level 1**: 0-1 AI use case (pilots)  
**Level 2**: 2-5 use cases (mostly pilots)  
**Level 3**: 5-10 use cases in production  
**Level 4**: 10-20+ use cases across functions  
**Level 5**: 20+ integrated use cases, competitive advantage  

### 6. Model Operations
How well are models managed in production?

**Level 1**: Manual model management, limited monitoring  
**Level 2**: Some documentation, basic monitoring, ad-hoc retraining  
**Level 3**: Documented lifecycle, regular monitoring, scheduled retraining  
**Level 4**: Automated lifecycle, advanced monitoring, continuous optimization  
**Level 5**: Fully automated operations, predictive monitoring, self-optimizing  

### 7. Business Impact & Value
What is the measurable business impact of AI?

**Level 1**: <$100K annual value  
**Level 2**: $100K-$1M annual value  
**Level 3**: $1M-$5M annual value  
**Level 4**: $5M-$20M annual value  
**Level 5**: >$20M annual value + competitive advantage  

## Scoring Methodology

**Process**
1. Review capability statements
2. Score each statement: 1 (not achieved) to 5 (fully achieved)
3. Average scores within each dimension
4. Calculate overall maturity: average of all dimensions
5. Compare to benchmark data

**Interpretation**
- **1.0-1.5**: Initial - Ad-hoc, no formal processes
- **1.5-2.5**: Developing - Some processes, emerging capability
- **2.5-3.5**: Defined - Documented processes, multiple initiatives
- **3.5-4.5**: Managed - Optimized processes, governance in place
- **4.5-5.0**: Optimized - Continuous improvement, strategic advantage

## Improvement Roadmap

### From Level 2 to Level 3 (6-12 months)

**Strategy**: Develop formal strategy, establish governance council  
**Data**: Implement data governance, centralize critical datasets  
**Talent**: Hire AI lead, establish center of excellence  
**Risk**: Develop risk framework, conduct bias assessments  
**Portfolio**: Move 2-3 pilots to production  
**Operations**: Document model standards, implement monitoring  
**Value**: Target $1M+ annual value  

### From Level 3 to Level 4 (12-18 months)

**Strategy**: Embed AI in all strategies, expand governance scope  
**Data**: Implement enterprise data platform, real-time governance  
**Talent**: Build AI team from 2-3 to 5-10 people, partnerships  
**Risk**: Implement advanced controls, continuous monitoring  
**Portfolio**: Expand to 10-15 use cases across functions  
**Operations**: Automate model lifecycle, advanced monitoring  
**Value**: Target $5M+ annual value  

### From Level 4 to Level 5 (18-24 months)

**Strategy**: AI-driven competitive advantage, board leadership  
**Data**: Real-time, self-governing data platform  
**Talent**: World-class AI center, 15-25 person team  
**Risk**: Predictive risk management, proactive controls  
**Portfolio**: 20+ integrated use cases, new capabilities  
**Operations**: Self-optimizing systems, minimal human intervention  
**Value**: >$20M annual value + strategic advantage  

## Implementation Planning

**Step 1**: Conduct assessment and identify gaps  
**Step 2**: Prioritize improvements (big impact, achievable)  
**Step 3**: Allocate resources (budget, people, executive sponsor)  
**Step 4**: Set milestones and measures  
**Step 5**: Execute and monitor progress  
**Step 6**: Reassess in 6-12 months  

## Benchmark Comparison

Use the benchmark data to:

- **Identify opportunities**: Where are you weakest vs. peers?
- **Justify investments**: Build business case for improvements
- **Track progress**: Measure progress against cohort

## Success Factors for Advancement

4. **Adequate budget** for tools and infrastructure
5. **Cross-functional alignment** (business, tech, risk)
6. **Governance** with clear decision authority
7. **Metrics** to measure progress and value
