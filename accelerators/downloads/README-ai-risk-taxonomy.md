## Overview

A comprehensive taxonomy of AI-specific risks in financial services, organized into Model, Data, Operational, and Regulatory categories. Includes risk register template and assessment methodology aligned with regulatory guidance.

## What's Included

- **Risk Categories**: Model, data, operational, and regulatory risk definitions
- **Risk Assessment Methodology**: Likelihood × Impact scoring framework
- **AI Risk Register Template**: Complete framework for documenting risks
- **Risk Indicators & Triggers**: Early warning signals for each risk category
- **Mitigation Strategies**: Proven approaches to reduce AI risks
- **Regulatory Alignment**: Mapping to Fed, OCC, and FDIC guidance

## Target Audience

- Chief Risk Officer

1. **Identify applicable risks** for each AI system using the taxonomy

5. **Implement mitigations** aligned with your risk appetite

## Key Risk Categories

**Model Risk**: Performance, bias, interpretability, stability  
**Data Risk**: Quality, bias, privacy, governance  
**Operational Risk**: Integration, governance, vendor, change management  
**Regulatory Risk**: Fair lending, consumer protection, explainability, compliance

## Risk Register Template

Each AI system should maintain a living risk register with:
- Risk ID and categorization
- Clear description of the risk
- Current state assessment (Green/Amber/Red)
- Likelihood and Impact scores
- Overall risk rating
- Owner and mitigation plan
- Timeline for remediation

## Regulatory Guidance Alignment

This taxonomy incorporates guidance from:
- **Federal Reserve SR 11-7**: Model Risk Management framework
- **OCC Bulletin 2020-4**: AI and ML Governance and Risk Management
- **FDIC**: Sound Practices for Model Risk Management
- **Interagency Guidance**: Fair Credit Reporting and Credit Risk Assessment Models

## Critical Risks in Financial Services

1. **Fair Lending Risk**: Discrimination based on protected characteristics
2. **Model Performance Risk**: Accuracy degradation in production
3. **Data Quality Risk**: Biased, incomplete, or unrepresentative training data
4. **Explainability Risk**: Unable to explain AI decisions to regulators
5. **Operational Risk**: AI system failures causing business disruption

## Implementation Approach

**Phase 1**: Risk identification workshops for each AI system  
**Phase 2**: Complete risk registers with baseline scoring  
**Phase 3**: Implement monitoring dashboards and alert systems  
**Phase 4**: Monthly risk assessment updates and trend analysis  

## Mitigation Framework

Risks are mitigated through a combination of:
- **Governance controls** (policies, approvals, oversight)
- **Technical controls** (testing, monitoring, validation)
- **Organizational controls** (training, procedures, escalation)
- **Compensating controls** (human review, sampling, audits)
